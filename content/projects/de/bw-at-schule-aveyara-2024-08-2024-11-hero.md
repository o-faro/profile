## Context

Development of a web application in the public sector, created to support digital workflows in an educational context within a regulated environment.

The project required compliance with accessibility standards and careful consideration of architectural tradeoffs under organizational and legal constraints.

---

## Role & setup

Senior Frontend Engineer (freelance), involved from early technical evaluation through implementation.

Close collaboration with stakeholders in a regulated public-sector context, with a strong focus on requirements clarification and long-term maintainability.

---

## Problem space

- Public sector environment with formal requirements and limited flexibility
- Mandatory compliance with WCAG and BITV accessibility standards
- Evaluation of architectural approaches under real-world constraints
- Need for predictable behavior, testability, and long-term maintainability
- Alignment of technical solutions with stakeholder expectations

---

## Key decisions & solutions

- Evaluation of static site generation versus client-side rendering in the given project context
- Conscious decision in favor of a client-side Next.js application based on functional and technical requirements
- Implementation of accessible UI components according to WCAG and BITV guidelines
- Emphasis on clear component structure and predictable UI behavior
- Setup of testing strategies to support long-term stability

---

## Quality & collaboration

- Continuous alignment with stakeholders on regulatory and functional requirements
- Strong focus on readable, maintainable code
- Pragmatic handling of constraints typical for public-sector projects

The application went live and is used in a production environment in compliance with accessibility and regulatory requirements.
